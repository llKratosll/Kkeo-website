The credit paradox

You can't get a score without a score.

A credit score is how landlords, banks and telcos decide if they can trust you. But if you've never had credit, you're invisible — not risky, just unseen. So you're asked for months of rent upfront, a guarantor, or pushed onto expensive prepaid plans.
